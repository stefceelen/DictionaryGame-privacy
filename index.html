Privacy Policy

Dictionary Game — Last updated: April 2026

Dictionary Game is a multiplayer word game for mobile devices. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and how it is handled.

1. Data We Collect

  • Player name — the name you choose before joining a game. This is entered by you and visible to other players in the same game.
  • Device ID — a randomly generated unique identifier (UUID) created the first time you open the app. It is stored locally on your device and used to identify you across sessions without requiring an account.
  • Game data — your submitted descriptions, votes, and scores within a game session.

2. Why We Collect It

  • To allow you to join and participate in multiplayer games.
  • To display your name and score to other players in the same game.
  • To remember your name and preferences between sessions.

3. How Data Is Stored

Your player name and device ID are stored locally on your device. Game data (descriptions, votes, scores, player name and device ID) is stored on our server. Old game data is automatically deleted after a period of time. We do not store payment information.

4. Third Parties

We do not share your data with third parties, sell your data, or use any advertising or analytics services.

5. Children

Dictionary Game is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.

6. Your Rights

Since the app does not require an account, no personal data is tied to your identity. You can reset your device ID at any time by uninstalling and reinstalling the app. If you have questions about your data, contact us at the address below.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves (for example, when in-app purchases are introduced). The latest version will always be available at this page.
